About

Fiorella Parra‑Mujica is a PhD candidate at the Erasmus School of Health Policy & Management in Rotterdam. Her research applies causal inference methods in health economics to understand how environmental shocks affect gendered health outcomes. Fiorella obtained an MSc in Health Economics (with distinction) from the University of York and a BA in Economics from Universidad del Pacífico in Peru.

She is particularly interested in poverty, inequalities and environmental determinants of health. Her current research examines how climatic shocks and air pollution influence maternal and child health in low‑ and middle‑income settings.

Research Experience

  • Researcher, Health Economics Research Centre, University of Oxford (Apr 2021 – Aug 2022)

  • Research Associate, EQUIPOL Group, University of York (Sept 2020 – Mar 2021)
